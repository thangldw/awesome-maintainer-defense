# AI-assisted contribution policy

AI tools may assist a contribution, but a human contributor must own the result.

## Requirements

Contributors must:

- read and understand every submitted change;
- verify that the change solves an accepted project problem;
- run the project's required tests and report the actual results;
- check generated code, citations, dependencies, licenses, and security-sensitive behavior;
- respond personally to review questions and revise the change when requested;
- disclose substantial automated assistance in the pull request when it affected implementation, tests, documentation, or security analysis.

Automated agents must not open unsolicited issues or pull requests without a responsible human who will participate in review. AI-generated vulnerability reports must include a reproducible proof, affected versions, impact analysis, and evidence that the reporter verified the result.

## Not accepted

Maintainers may close contributions that are unrequested, cannot be reproduced, contain fabricated APIs or citations, add broad unrelated changes, restate an issue without new evidence, or require more review effort than their likely value.

The decision is based on quality, project fit, and contributor responsiveness—not on an attempt to prove which tool wrote the text.

## Enforcement and appeal

The usual first response is a request for evidence or a smaller change. Repeated low-quality submissions may be closed without detailed review. Spam, harassment, impersonation, or evasion of repository controls may result in blocking and a report to the hosting platform.

If a legitimate contribution is incorrectly labeled or closed, reply once with the missing reproduction, test results, and a concise explanation of project fit. A maintainer will review the new evidence when capacity permits.
